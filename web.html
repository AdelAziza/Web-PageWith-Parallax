My Passion for technology

Since I was a little girl, I usually want to be the first girl to have high education in my family. Coming from a culture where women are born to get married and give children, it has been a challenge for me to travel and be educated. However, my parents are working hard so that I can have a better education and encourage me to achieve my goals. I finished my high school in 2015. Although the tradition believes about women, I want to distinguish myself in my community by having a degree in Web development and become a teacher of programming that are the career goals that I want to obtain. I came from a country where I did not have the chance to learn how to use and manipulate computers, so I became interested in learning how computers work. Through that experience increase my desire to learn about computers and learn to be an expert female. How have my life experiences influenced why I want to go to college?

Many girls in my community did not work to school even going finishing high school. However, one of my cousins was able to attend high school, but when she was in 10th Grade, her parents were not able to pay for her education. She had no other choice than staying at home and taking care of her siblings. However, her parents were working hard to found money for her education. After weeks, my cousin decided to get married at 17 years old. I was devastating, confused and frustrated because I was ten years old and I was worried about my situation because we were 8 in my family. I knew how tuitions were expensive. It was regrettable for all of us who were looking to her as a big sister who could have been a mentor for all of us. Despite the high cost of tuition, my parents were able to pay for all of us. That experience was a reinforcement on my determination on achieving my dreams which is to be an independent woman who can be an example for other girls who continue to struggle on how to execute their purposes. It motivates me to study something that I can use in the future to help others by being a professor. I learned from that experience that it is still possible to create other alternative ways to achieve your goals. It is better to suffer now for a short period of time in order to have a mindset to continue fighting for your dreams to be a reality. Spring 2017, I took a programming class, and it was my first time to code. It was challenging for me to translate each word the teacher was saying in my native language for a better understanding. I was timid in the class due to my lack of vocabulary in English, and I was not able to ask questions to my professor. As a consequent, I failed to the first project with a D. I was a disappointment to myself. My professor emailed me and asked me if I could have time to meet with him. I was scared and did not know what to tell him about my performance on the exam. I took the courage to meet him, and the discussion helped me realize that I was surrounded by many supports that I can use to be successful. I guided me to the Academic Success Center where they are tutors who can help me to understand other concepts since sometimes in class we did not have enough time. After, two weeks of hard work and meeting once a week with my professor after class helped me to understand better. On the second exam, I had an A, and I was proud of myself. I learned that with hard work and determination, you could achieve your goals. It is essential to learn from a failure to avoid making the same mistakes in the future.
